Problem Statement

The Challenge in E-Commerce

With massive product catalogs, customers struggle to find related accessories, resulting in lower transaction values.

  • Manual bundling strategies are slow and bias-prone.
  • Standard item pairing systems require massive computing structures.
  • Real-time calculations can add page load latency, driving away users.

The Goal

Automate cross-selling by mining database transaction patterns to instantly recommend products that customers are statistically most likely to buy together.

Core Algorithm

Market Basket Analysis & Apriori

Market Basket Analysis (MBA) explores association rules that show how strongly items are connected in a transaction database.

The Apriori Algorithm is an unsupervised learning method that isolates sets of items that appear together frequently by weeding out rare combinations.

  • Scans transactional datasets.
  • Builds confidence metrics for pairs and bundles.
  • Extracts mathematical rules to drive checkout suggestions.

Mathematical Foundations

The Three pillars of Association Rules

📊

1. Support

Freq(A, B) / N

Measures how frequently the combination of items occurs. It controls the baseline popularity of a rule.

📈

2. Confidence

Support(A, B) / Support(A)

Measures the conditional probability of purchase. If a customer buys A, what is the chance they buy B?

🚀

3. Lift

Confidence(A→B) / Support(B)

Determines rule strength. A lift > 1 means buying A boosts the rate of buying B compared to normal behavior.

Data Engineering

Conditional Probability Dataset

The system generates exactly 500 realistic shopping carts containing product categories mapped with specific bundling behaviors:

  • Laptop Rule: Mouse (70%), Laptop Bag (60%), Keyboard (40%).
  • Smartphone Rule: Charger (85%), Screen Protector (80%), Earbuds (50%).
  • DSLR Camera Rule: SD Card (75%), Tripod (65%), Camera Bag (55%).
  • Adds 15 additional catalog items as transaction noise.

API Architecture

Flask Serverless Backend

The backend is built with Python and Flask. It separates modeling from execution to guarantee immediate load times.

  • Offline Training: Model is trained once, and association rules are serialized to model.pkl.
  • Inference speed: At runtime, the API loads model.pkl to run lookups in O(1) time instead of retraining.
  • Endpoints: Supports health diagnostics at /health and queries at POST /recommend.
