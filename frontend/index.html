Understanding Association Rules & Apriori

Market Basket Analysis runs on the Apriori algorithm to identify sets of products that frequently co-occur in historical transaction records.

Support

Support(A) = Transactions containing A / Total Transactions

Measures the popularity of a product set. A support of 0.05 means the product appears in 5% of all transactions analyzed.

Confidence

Confidence(A → B) = Support(A ∪ B) / Support(A)

Indicates how often the rule is correct. A confidence of 0.70 means 70% of customers who bought A also bought B.

Lift

Lift(A → B) = Confidence(A → B) / Support(B)

Measures the strength of the rule. A lift > 1 indicates that buying A significantly increases the likelihood of buying B compared to B's baseline popularity.
